Applied to microphone before it enters the mix bus. Keeps mic clean without touching music.
HIGHPASS 80 Hz
Removes handling noise, breath and rumble below 80 Hz
NOISE GATE −45 dB

Hard ceiling prevents mic from overloading the mix bus

Mic enters the mix bus and passes through Master DSP chain
🎛 MASTER DSP CHAIN — FINAL SPEC▸
Professional radio console chain. Fixed −4 dB trim isolates DSP from slider movement — sliders control loudness only, DSP controls sound character only. Stereo locked throughout (channelCount=2, explicit).
CHANNEL GAINS
Deck A/B 81% · Cart 71% · Mic 92% · Master 100% — calibrated unity levels per spec
MAIN MIX BUS
All channels sum here. Target: peak ≈ −6 dBFS, average −12 to −10 dBFS
DUCK BUS
Rides music (Deck A/B) −12 dB when mic or cart fires. Cart injects after the duck bus — never self-ducked. Cart plays at full level while music ducks underneath
FIXED TRIM −4 dB (×0.631)
Constant trim between mix bus and DSP. Never user-adjustable. Keeps DSP input stable regardless of fader positions
HIGHPASS 35 Hz
Removes sub-bass below 35 Hz — eliminates DC offset and rumble, preserves full kick and bass character
EQ: +2 dB @ 90 Hz
Body and punch — kick drum and bass note centre frequency, compensates for bass rolloff on earbuds
EQ: +2.5 dB @ 3 kHz
Presence and intelligibility — vocal consonants, guitar bite, snare crack. Reproduced by every device
EQ: +2 dB @ 10 kHz
Air and brilliance — open top end on monitors and earbuds, compensates for HF rolloff on Bluetooth speakers
SOFT CLIPPER — drive 0.11
Algebraic saturation · 2x oversample · rounds peaks, adds broadcast glue — barely audible at this drive
BASS MONO — crossover 120 Hz
Below 120 Hz: L+R summed to mono (×0.5). Above 120 Hz: full stereo preserved. Eliminates bass phase cancellation on mono devices, tightens low end on monitors
LIMITER −0.3 dB — occasional only
Fires only on inter-sample peaks above −0.3 dBFS · knee 0.5 dB · 1ms attack · 100ms release · ratio 20:1. Silent on normal programme material
MASTER GAIN (user fader)
Output volume only — post-DSP. Moving master never affects tone, EQ or compression behaviour

🏠  1. OVERVIEW & GETTING STARTED►

Eyethu Pro Radio Studio is a fully self-contained, single-file Progressive Web App (PWA) designed for professional radio automation. Runs entirely in your browser with no server, no installation, and no internet required after the first load.

Works on Android phones, tablets, desktop and laptop computers. Install as a PWA for an app-like experience with offline support.

STUDIODual-deck playback, queue, 12-cart cartwall, mic panel, 5-channel console
LIBRARYImport and manage your music — persists across sessions automatically
SCHEDULEAutomated shows with playlists, ad breaks, overlays, news breaks and weather breaks
SETTINGSDSP chain, fader defaults & presets, repeat guard, audio device routing
NEWSLive headlines via APITube or Currents API — ElevenLabs reads them on air
WEATHERUp to 5 cities via OpenWeatherMap — ElevenLabs reads conditions on air
STATIONSSearch and play 50,000+ internet radio stations on Deck A or B via Radio Browser
METADATAAuto-enriches tracks with artist, album, genre and year via MusicBrainz
💡 First time? Go to Library → tap 📂 IMPORT FILES to load your music. Then go to Studio, enable AUTO-DJ: ON and press ▶ on Deck A to start broadcasting.
🎙  2. STUDIO TAB►

The Studio tab is your main broadcast surface. Deck A and Deck B are pinned to the top in both portrait and landscape — they stay visible at all times while the queue, cartwall, mic panel and console scroll below.

► DECK A & DECK B
📂Load a single audio file directly onto this deck

Progress barTap anywhere to seek to that position
► QUEUE & AUTO-DJ

The queue holds tracks in playback order. Tap any Library track to add it. Auto-DJ loads the next track onto the opposite deck when a song ends, alternating A → B → A seamlessly. Played tracks rotate to the end so playback loops indefinitely. Tracks start only when enough audio is buffered — no crackle on track starts.

AUTO-DJ: ON/OFFToggle automatic next-track loading and playback
🔀Shuffle the queue into a random order
✕ CLEARRemove all tracks from the queue
Repeat GuardPrevents the same track replaying within a set lockout window (configure in Settings)
► CARTWALL (12 slots)

12 instant-play cart buttons for jingles, station IDs, ads, news jingles and weather stingers. Each cart bypasses the duck bus — carts play at full level while music ducks −12 dB underneath. Carts never duck themselves.

Tap oncePlay the cart — music ducks −12 dB automatically
Tap againStop the cart — music restores over 800ms
📂 buttonLoad an audio file onto that cart slot
F1–F12 keysDesktop: trigger carts 1–12 from keyboard (play/stop toggle)
► MICROPHONE & ON AIR

The mic routes through a pre-processing chain (HPF 80 Hz → Noise Gate → Safety Limiter) and connects directly to the main mix bus after the duck bus — mic is never ducked by its own signal. Off-air, mic gain is gated to zero regardless of the fader position.

🎤 MIC ON/OFFRequest mic access and toggle the pre-processing chain
🔴 ON AIRMutes studio monitors, opens mic live, activates −12 dB auto-ducking on music
⚪ OFF AIRRestores monitors, gates mic to standby, releases ducking over 800ms
► CHANNEL CONSOLE

5-channel mixer (Deck A, Deck B, Mic, Cart, Master) with vertical faders and live VU meters. Faders are bi-directionally linked with the deck/mic sliders. Default levels: Deck A/B 81%, Mic 92%, Cart 71%, Master 100%.

MUTESilences the channel instantly — saves pre-mute level, restores on tap. Mute states survive page reloads. A muted MIC stays silent even ON AIR.
F1–F12Desktop: trigger carts 1–12
Space / PDesktop: play / pause active deck
📂  3. MUSIC LIBRARY►

The Library is your track database. Both track metadata and audio data are automatically saved between sessions — metadata in localStorage, audio files in IndexedDB. Everything restores on next open without re-importing.

► MusicBrainz Enrichment

Go to the METADATA tab and enable AUTO LOOKUP — every track loaded to a deck is automatically enriched with artist, album, genre and year from MusicBrainz. No API key required. Tap ENRICH ALL TRACKS to batch-enrich your entire library.

📅  4. SHOW SCHEDULER►

Pre-program shows that start and stop automatically at set times. Each show has its own music playlist, ad breaks, overlays, news breaks and weather breaks. The scheduler uses an accurate WorldTimeAPI-synced clock to prevent drift on tablets.

► Creating a Show
START / END24-hour clock — supports overnight shows (e.g. 23:00–01:00)
RECURRENCEDaily · Weekly (day chips) · Specific date · Monthly · Once only
INTRO / OUTROOptional cart to play at show start and end
ORDERIn Sequence · Random · Weighted Random (higher-weight tracks play more often)
► Break Types — all support 3 trigger modes
🔊 OVERLAYDucks music −12 dB, plays sweeper over it — music continues underneath
⏸ AD BREAKPauses music, plays ad, resumes from exactly where it stopped
📰 NEWS BREAKSong fades out → jingle → ElevenLabs reads headlines → next song starts fresh. Auto-fetches from APITube / Currents before reading.
🌤 WEATHER BREAKSong fades out → jingle → ElevenLabs reads up to 5 cities → next song starts. Auto-fetches from OpenWeatherMap. Per-show city override supported.
► Trigger Modes (for all break types)
After N songsFires after every N tracks played
Every X minutesFires at regular intervals from show start
At HH:MMFires once at an exact clock time within the show
💡 Conflict protection: Ad, Overlay, News and Weather breaks never fire simultaneously. If two triggers would overlap, the second reschedules automatically.
⚙️  5. SETTINGS►
► Master DSP Chain (fixed, post-mix bus)

All sources pass through this chain in series. Sliders control loudness only — DSP controls sound character only. The two are fully isolated by a fixed −4 dB trim node.

FIXED TRIM −4 dBIsolates DSP from slider movement — moving a fader never changes tone or processing behaviour
HIGHPASS 35 HzRemoves sub-bass rumble and DC offset from the full mix
EQ +2 dB @ 90 HzBody and punch — kick drum and bass weight
EQ +2.5 dB @ 3 kHzPresence and intelligibility — vocal consonants, cut-through on any speaker
EQ +2 dB @ 10 kHzAir and brilliance — open top end, compensates for Bluetooth HF rolloff
SOFT CLIPPER 0.11Algebraic saturation, 2x oversample — rounds peaks, adds broadcast glue
BASS MONO 120 HzBelow 120 Hz: mono. Above: full stereo. Eliminates bass phase cancellation on phones and BT speakers.
LIMITER −0.3 dBSafety net only — fires on inter-sample peaks above −0.3 dBFS. Silent on normal programme material.
► Duck Bus

Only Deck A and Deck B pass through the duck bus. Mic and Cart connect directly after it, so neither ever ducks its own signal. Duck level: −12 dB, 15ms attack, 800ms release.

► Fader Defaults & Presets

Default levels: Deck A/B 81% · Mic 92% · Cart 71% · Master 100%. Save and name up to unlimited presets (stored in localStorage) — recall any preset with one tap.

► Audio Device Routing

Route the master output to any connected audio interface or virtual cable (Chrome 110+ / Edge 110+ required). Monitor output can be routed separately from headphone output for split-cue monitoring.

🌤  7. WEATHER TAB►

Live weather conditions for up to 5 cities, read on air by ElevenLabs. Free OpenWeatherMap plan gives 1,000 calls/day. Integrates with the Show Scheduler for timed weather breaks.

► Setup
OWM KEYOpenWeatherMap API key — free, no credit card. Register at openweathermap.org → API Keys.
CITIES 1–5Enter any city names. City 1 is required — Cities 2–5 are optional. Leave blank to skip.
UNITSCelsius (°C) or Fahrenheit (°F)
JINGLECart to play before the weather is read — ideal for a "Weather with Eyethu Radio" stinger
REFRESH / AUTOAuto-fetches all cities on a timer. Fetching every 60 min uses 5 × 24 = 120 credits/day — well within 1,000 free.
► Weather Break Flow

Song fades out over 5 seconds → optional jingle → ElevenLabs reads all cities in one sentence → sign-off → next song starts fresh. Example: "And now for the weather. Johannesburg 19 degrees partly cloudy, Cape Town 24 sunny, Durban 28 humid. That's your weather update."

► Per-Show City Override

In each show's WEATHER BREAK section, enter a comma-separated city list to override the station default for that show only. Leave blank to use the station default.

💡 API credits: 5 cities × 1 req each = 5 credits per fetch. Auto every 60 min = 120 credits/day. Free tier: 1,000 credits/day.
📻  8. STATIONS TAB►

Search and play 50,000+ internet radio stations from Radio Browser — no API key, no signup, completely free. Filter by name and country. Play any station directly on Deck A or B through the full DSP chain.

Search fieldType a station name, genre or city — press Enter or tap SEARCH
Country filterNarrows results to a specific country (South Africa, Nigeria, Kenya, etc.)
A / B buttonsLoad and immediately play the station on Deck A or Deck B

Stations stream through crossOrigin: anonymous so they are correctly captured into the Web Audio graph — the stream passes through Deck gain → mix bus → DSP chain → master output exactly like local files.

💡 Use the STATIONS tab to relay another South African station during a break, fill dead air between shows, or monitor competition.
🎵  9. METADATA TAB►

MusicBrainz metadata enrichment — no API key required. Looks up artist, album, genre and year from your track filenames. Enriched data is saved to the track object and persists across sessions.

AUTO LOOKUPEvery track loaded to a deck is automatically looked up. Results appear in the NOW PLAYING card.
LOOKUP NOWManually look up the currently playing track
ENRICH ALL TRACKSBatch-enriches every track in your library at 1 request/second (MusicBrainz rate limit). Progress shown live.
Genre tagsDisplayed as coloured chips — useful for planning playlists and show formats
💡 For best results, name your files clearly: Artist – Track Title.mp3 gives MusicBrainz the best chance of finding a match.
