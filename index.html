🏠  1. OVERVIEW & GETTING STARTED▸

Eyethu Pro Radio Studio is a fully self-contained, single-file Progressive Web App (PWA) designed for professional and semi-professional radio automation. It runs entirely inside your web browser — no server, no installation, no internet connection required after the first load.

It works on Android phones, tablets, desktop and laptop computers. On Android Chrome and desktop browsers it can be installed to your home screen or desktop like a native app using the install banner that appears at the bottom of the screen.

STUDIODual-deck playback, crossfader, queue, cartwall, mic, console mixer
LIBRARYImport individual files or an entire music folder
SCHEDULEAutomated show scheduling with intro/outro carts and ad breaks
SETTINGSDSP chain, fader defaults, presets, device routing, this manual
💡 First time? Go to the Library tab → tap 📁 OPEN FOLDER to load your music, then head to Studio and tap AUTO-DJ: ON. Press ▶ on Deck A to start broadcasting.
🎛  2. STUDIO TAB▸

The Studio tab is your main broadcast control surface. It contains the dual decks, crossfader, queue, cartwall, microphone, ON AIR switch and channel console.

▸ DECK A & DECK B
📂Load a single audio file from your device directly onto this deck
▶ / ⏸Play or pause the loaded track
■Stop and return to the beginning
CUESet a cue point at the current playback position
↩Jump back to the saved cue point
⏏Eject — unloads the track and frees memory
VOLDeck volume fader — also reflected in the console strip
Progress barTap anywhere on the bar to seek to that position
VU Meter20-segment LED meter with peak hold — green/yellow/red zones
▸ CROSSFADER

The crossfader blends between Deck A (left) and Deck B (right) using constant-power curves so the mix stays at full volume throughout. Deck volume faders are independent of the crossfader — you can use both together.

⟺ AUTO XFADESmoothly slides the crossfader to the opposite side over 3 seconds
▸ QUEUE & AUTO-DJ

The queue holds tracks in the order they'll be played. Tap any track in the Library to add it. Auto-DJ watches for a track to finish and automatically loads the next queue item onto the opposite deck, then plays it — creating a seamless alternating A/B playback loop.

AUTO-DJ: ON/OFFToggle automatic next-track loading and playback
🔀Shuffle the queue into a random order
✕ CLEARRemove all tracks from the queue
▾ / ▸Collapse or expand the queue to save screen space
▸ CARTWALL

8 instant-play cart buttons for jingles, stingers, station IDs, sound effects and ad spots. Each cart auto-ducks the music when it plays and restores the level when it finishes.

TapPlay the cart (restarts if already playing)
📂 iconLoad an audio file onto that cart slot
Long-pressAlso opens the load dialog on mobile
▾ / ▸Collapse or expand the cartwall
▸ MICROPHONE
🎤 MIC ONRequest mic access and activate the mic chain
🔄 RECONNECTReconnect mic if the browser disconnected it
VOLMic pre-gain — also reflected in the M channel on the console
AUTO-DUCKMusic automatically ducks when the mic is live (ON AIR mode only)

▸ ON AIR / OFF AIR
🔴 ON AIRActivates broadcast mode — monitors cut, mic goes live to mix bus, auto-duck enabled
⚪ OFF AIRReturns to monitor mode — mic muted, speakers restored
▸ CHANNEL CONSOLE

A 5-channel mixer with vertical faders and live VU meters for Deck A, Deck B, Mic, Cart and Master. All faders are bi-directionally linked — moving a fader on the console also moves the corresponding deck/mic slider and updates the actual audio gain in real time.

MASTEROverall output trim — sits after the DSP chain
💡 The console channel meters are also segmented LED strips — green = safe, yellow = approaching hot, red = clipping risk. A white-outline peak dot holds briefly after peaks.

The Scheduler lets you pre-program shows that start and stop automatically at set times. Each show has its own playlist, ad break settings and intro/outro carts.

⚙️  5. SETTINGS▸
▸ Master DSP Chain

The master DSP chain is a 12-stage professional audio processing pipeline applied to all audio before it reaches the output. The chain is always active and processes in this order:

HIGHPASSRemoves sub-bass rumble below 35 Hz (adjustable 20–120 Hz)
AGC LEVELERAuto Gain Control — keeps loud and quiet tracks at consistent loudness
DENSITY COMP3.5:1 compressor — tightens dynamics for a punchy broadcast sound
3-BAND MULTIIndependent low/mid/high band compression for tonal balance
BASS EQ90 Hz low-shelf — adds warmth and weight
PRESENCE EQ3 kHz peaking — adds clarity and cut-through
AIR EQ9 kHz high-shelf — adds brightness and air
STEREO WIDTH×1.05 mid-side width enhancement
SOFT CLIPPER0.7 threshold — gentle harmonic saturation before limiting
PSYCHO LOUD+5% perceptual loudness boost
LOOK-AHEAD LIM5ms look-ahead transparent limiter
BRICKWALL LIMHard ceiling at −0.3 dBFS — prevents digital clipping absolutely
▸ Mic DSP Chain

The mic chain is a 9-stage processing pipeline applied only to the microphone input, active only when ON AIR.

HIGHPASS80 Hz filter — removes handling noise, breath and rumble
NOISE GATE−45 dB threshold — silences background noise between speech
DE-ESSER6 kHz dynamic notch — tames harsh sibilance (S and T sounds)
COMPRESSOR3:1 ratio — controls mic dynamics for a consistent voice level
WARMTH EQ120 Hz low-shelf +2 dB — adds body and warmth to the voice
PRESENCE EQ3 kHz peaking +2 dB — adds clarity and intelligibility
MIC LIMITER−4 dB threshold — prevents mic peaks from clipping
MIC DUCKINGLowers music level while mic is live — depth and release adjustable
→ MIX BUSSends processed mic signal to the master mix — ON AIR only
▸ Default Fader Levels & Presets

Default fader levels are the starting volumes for all channels. Moving a default slider instantly applies that level to the live fader, the console strip and the audio gain node — everything stays in sync.

DECK A / BDefault: 80% — leaves headroom for mixing
MICDefault: 90% — high sensitivity for voice
CARTDefault: 80% — balanced for jingles and ads
MASTERDefault: 100% — full output after DSP chain

Presets: Type a name in the preset box and tap 💾 SAVE to store the current default levels. Tap ▶ LOAD on any saved preset to recall it — all faders jump to those levels immediately. Presets survive page reloads.

▸ Audio Device Routing

On desktop browsers you can route different channels to different audio output devices. Tap ↺ REFRESH DEVICES to enumerate available outputs, then select the device for each channel from the dropdowns.

💡 Device routing requires the browser to have been granted audio permission. On Android, only the system default output is typically available.

📊  7. VU METERS & LEVELS▸

All meters in Eyethu Pro are 20-segment LED-style VU meters modelled on professional broadcast consoles. They display RMS (average) level with a slow-decay peak-hold indicator.

GREEN (0–65%)Normal operating level — safe zone
YELLOW (65–85%)Approaching hot — reduce level slightly if sustained
RED (85–100%)High level — the brickwall limiter will engage here
PEAK DOTWhite-outline segment that holds briefly at the peak level, then decays
💡 Aim to keep levels in the green-to-low-yellow range during normal music playback. The DSP brickwall limiter protects against clipping, but sustained red levels will cause audible limiting artefacts.
📱  8. PWA INSTALLATION & MOBILE TIPS▸

Eyethu Pro is a Progressive Web App — it can be installed directly to your Android home screen, iOS home screen, or Windows/Mac desktop for a full-screen, native-app-like experience.

▸ Installing on Android Chrome

An install banner appears automatically at the bottom of the screen on first load. Tap ⬇ INSTALL APP to add it to your home screen. Alternatively, tap the browser menu (⋮) → Add to Home screen.

▸ Installing on iOS Safari

Tap the Share button (□↑) at the bottom of Safari → Add to Home Screen → Add. The app will open full-screen without browser UI.

▸ Installing on Desktop (Chrome/Edge)

Click the install icon (⊕) in the browser address bar, or go to browser menu → Install Eyethu Radio.

▸ Mobile Tips
Landscape modeRotate your device — the layout adapts to fill the wider screen with 2-column sections
Screen stay-onEnable "Keep screen on" in your phone's display settings to prevent Auto-DJ from stopping
Audio on AndroidTap once anywhere (e.g. deck ▶ button) before using Auto-DJ — Android requires a user gesture to unlock the AudioContext
Mic permissionThe first time you tap MIC ON, the browser will ask for microphone permission — tap Allow
Folder importOn Android Chrome, tap 📁 OPEN FOLDER → browse to your music folder and confirm selection

▸ Library Quick Actions
Tap trackAdd to queue

▸ Collapse / Expand Cards

The Queue, Cartwall and Microphone cards all have a ▾/▸ button in their header. Tap the header or the arrow to collapse that section and reclaim screen space — especially useful on phones in portrait mode.

▸ Saving State

The app auto-saves your state (library metadata, queue, shows, DSP settings, fader positions, presets) to the browser's localStorage every time you make a change. State is restored automatically on next open.

▸ Audio Formats Supported

MP3, WAV, FLAC, AAC, M4A, OGG, OPUS, WebA, WMA, AIFF — support depends on your browser, but MP3, WAV and AAC work on all platforms.
